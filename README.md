---

## 🎯 Project Overview

Real businesses don't store data the way analysts need it. This project tackles that gap: starting from a messy relational database of rental transactions, I designed a data warehouse from scratch and built Tableau dashboards that answer real business questions about film performance, revenue, and customer behavior.

---

## 📈 What I Tracked

| Business Question | Metric |
|---|---|
| Which films drive the most revenue? | Total revenue per title & category |
| Where are our best customers located? | Rentals & revenue by country and city |
| How long do customers keep films? | Average rental duration |
| Which genres perform best over time? | Monthly rental trends by category |
| Is our inventory working hard enough? | Rental count vs. replacement cost |

---

## 💡 What I Did

- **Designed a data warehouse** — consolidated 6+ relational tables (films, rentals, payments, customers, locations, categories) into a single analytics-ready table using SQL
- **Engineered business metrics** — computed aggregate stats including total rentals, total revenue, average rental duration, and actor count per film
- **Built Tableau dashboards** — created interactive visualizations to surface trends in film popularity, regional performance, and revenue over time
- **Treated it as a real business case** — scoped the warehouse around one focused subject (film performance) to ensure every metric served a clear business purpose
